Problem Statement
Domain
Cricket strategy & coaching technology
Background
Field placement in cricket is one of the most nuanced decisions a captain makes. It depends on:
The batsman's weaknesses (e.g., weak against short balls, drives wide of mid-on)
The bowler's type (pace, swing, spin, angle of delivery)
The match situation (powerplay, death overs, defensive set)
The pitch and conditions
Currently, captains rely on:
Personal memory and experience
Coach input
Gut instinct
There is no fast, visual, interactive decision-support tool that a coach, analyst, or even a fan can use to explore field placement strategies.
---
Problem Statement
> **"How can we help cricket captains and coaches make faster, better-reasoned field placement decisions using a visual, interactive tool?"**
---
Who Is This For?
User	Use Case
Amateur cricket captain	Quick field setup during match
Cricket coach	Teaching field placements to players
Cricket analyst	Exploring what-if scenarios
Fantasy cricket fan	Understanding team strategy
---
Pain Points
No visual tool exists for real-time field planning
Placement rationale is rarely documented or explained
Coaches can't quickly compare two fielding strategies
Young players don't learn why fielders are placed where they are
---
Opportunity
A lightweight web tool with a visual cricket oval + smart placement logic fills this gap entirely. It can be used on a phone or tablet at the boundary.
